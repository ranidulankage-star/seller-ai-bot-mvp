Seller AI Bot — MVP Entry Point Strategy

Should v1 live inside the Analytics tab, or debut as a top-level feature? This document mocks both paths side-by-side to make the tradeoff concrete.

June 2026 Whatnot Seller Hub AI · Analytics · MVP Scoping

Option 1 — Analytics-Scoped

The bot lives entirely within the Analytics tab. Sellers discover it only when they're already looking at data. Scope of answers matches scope of placement. Ship faster, measure easily, iterate in a contained surface.

Option 2 — Top-Level from Day 1

A persistent sparkle icon in the top bar + FAB signals a first-class product. Even if v1 only answers analytics questions, the entry point sets up the agentic future and trains a global interaction habit.

The real question: Does shipping a globally-accessible bot that can only answer analytics questions undermine trust and create confusion — or does it correctly signal intent and earn placement early? Option 1 avoids that question entirely. Option 2 requires the product to earn its spot fast.

Recommendation

Based on the mocks above, here's the clearest way to think about the choice.

Choose Option 1 (Analytics-Scoped) if...

You want to ship fast and prove value before expanding scope
The team doesn't yet have a strong out-of-analytics answer for cross-page AI questions
You want clean success metrics (e.g. "% of Analytics sessions that use AI")
Entry point 1B (contextual hover buttons) can be added to individual charts with minimal eng lift
You're comfortable promoting the AI surface to top-level in v2 once it covers 3+ page types

Choose Option 2 (Top-Level) if...

Leadership is committed to a public "Whatnot AI" narrative and the bot is a flagship launch
You have a strong graceful-degradation answer for cross-page questions (e.g. proactive handoff copy)
The analytics-only v1 will expand to 2+ more capabilities within 6–8 weeks of launch
You're willing to accept some early trust damage if the feature scope is narrower than the placement implies
Suggested path: Ship v1 with Option 1 entry points — specifically the combination of 1A (Ask AI tab) + 1B (inline chart buttons). This covers the full analytics surface with zero discovery ambiguity. When v2 expands to inventory or show planning, graduate the bot to a top-level FAB + top bar icon at that point — the re-education cost is low because sellers already have a mental model of "AI answers questions about my store data." The risk of shipping Option 2 with an analytics-only v1 is that sellers who open the bot from the Orders or Inventory page will receive a graceful deflection, and repeated deflections erode the trust that makes the global placement valuable in the first place.

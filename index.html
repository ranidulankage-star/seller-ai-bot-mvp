Entry Point Strategy

Seller AI Bot — Entry Point Decision

The MVP already ships as Analytics → Chat tab. This document asks: is that the right long-term placement, or do we elevate it to a top-level feature now? Mocks both paths with tradeoffs.

June 2026 Whatnot Seller Hub AI · Analytics · Entry Point ↗ Full vision spec
Current state — shipping now
Analytics Chat tab "Ask about your business"
whatnot.com/dashboard/analytics/chat
The bot is a tab within Analytics. Scoped, discoverable only to sellers already looking at analytics, but clearly low prominence. The question is whether to keep it here or graduate it.

Option 1 — Stay analytics-scoped

Improve the existing entry point — better tab design, inline chart buttons, persistent composer. Keep the bot inside Analytics for v1. Graduate it to top-level when scope expands.

Option 2 — Elevate to top-level now

Move the entry point to a global sparkle icon + FAB accessible from anywhere. Even though v1 only answers analytics questions, the placement signals the full agentic vision and avoids having to re-educate sellers later.

The real question: Does shipping a globally-accessible bot that can only answer analytics questions undermine trust — or does it correctly signal the agentic future and earn placement early? Option 1 is the safe, credible path. Option 2 requires the product to earn its top-level spot fast.

The core tradeoff — top-level entry with analytics-only v1
What goes right
✓ One consistent entry point that never needs to move
✓ Sets up the agentic future — no re-education when CX/inventory capabilities ship
✓ When sellers open it from Analytics, the experience is seamless and fast
✓ Signals "first-class AI product" from day one
What goes wrong
✗ Seller on Inventory opens FAB → "I can only help with analytics right now" → trust erodes
✗ The FAB/icon is present on 5 pages but only useful on 1 — creates false expectations
✗ Harder to attribute success: is low usage because the feature is weak, or because placement is wrong?
✗ Graceful deflection copy only goes so far — repeated dead ends damage the product's reputation
Mitigation if going top-level: Add a subtle "Analytics only · v1" scope tag inside the panel whenever opened from a non-analytics page. This sets expectations without requiring a separate entry point strategy.

Recommendation

Based on the mocks above, here's the clearest way to think about the choice.

Stay with Option 1 (analytics-scoped) if...

You want to ship fast and prove value before expanding scope
The team doesn't yet have a strong out-of-analytics answer for cross-page AI questions
You want clean success metrics (e.g. "% of Analytics sessions that use AI")
Entry point 1B (contextual hover buttons) can be added to individual charts with minimal eng lift
You're comfortable promoting the AI surface to top-level in v2 once it covers 3+ page types

Elevate to Option 2 (top-level) if...

Leadership is committed to a public "Whatnot AI" narrative and the bot is a flagship launch
You have a strong graceful-degradation answer for cross-page questions (e.g. proactive handoff copy)
The analytics-only v1 will expand to 2+ more capabilities within 6–8 weeks of launch
You're willing to accept some early trust damage if the feature scope is narrower than the placement implies
Suggested path: The current Analytics → Chat tab is the right place to start — but improve the tab itself with inline chart buttons (1B) to make the bot feel like a native part of the analytics experience, not an afterthought. When capabilities expand beyond analytics (CX, inventory, scheduling), graduate to Option 2 at that point. The re-education cost will be low — sellers will already have a mental model of "AI that answers questions about my store." The risk of jumping to Option 2 now is that sellers opening the bot from Orders or Inventory will hit repeated deflections, which erodes trust faster than low placement ever could.
